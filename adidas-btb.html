Break The Box

Problem: Not enough teenage girls (age 14-19) in London partake in the sport boxing.
Insight: Young girls are pressured by men into a box of societal norms, beauty standards and feminine traits.
Solution: Show girls breaking out of this box, which symbolises the gendered standards that have been created by men.

Client: Adidas
Status: Idea

Girls fighting back and breaking the male implemented social expectations through the medium of boxing. ‘Breaking the box’ through taster sessions.

Executions

Posters and billboards in and around schools such as school changing rooms and hallways, bus stops and shops to reach the young target audience and entice them to sign up for the free boxing taster sessions.

TV Script

Boys aged 14-19 are standing in front of a plain box.

Off camera:
Write what words come to mind when I say ‘girls’ or ‘feminine’.

Show the boys writing and the words that have been written. Image changes to a girl standing in front of the inscribed box.

Off camera:
Can you read out what has been written?

Girl reads out what is on the box.

Off camera:
Do you agree?

Expected response:
Don’t agree, upset ect.

Girls given boxing gloves to punch/break the box.

On screen text:
We don’t fit the box made for us!
Adidas has partnered with London boxing clubs to give girls taster sessions.
